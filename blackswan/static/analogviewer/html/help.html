Description:
This application retrieves the analogue counts for a selected period of interest (on the order of year, month or day) from a pre-calculated file generated by the Analogues Detection process or obtainable from the A2C2 website. Analogue counts are binned according to decade, correlation and distance. All charts are interactive and linked together so that a selection made in one chart will automatically update all the other charts. To cancel any filters, click on "reset". To cancel all filters, click on "Reset All".

Selection of the period of interest within the analysis period at a monthly resolution. Click on the chart with the mouse to define a selection brush.

You can also filter by season or combination of seasons using the pie chart.
You can zoom on the chart with the mouse wheel to the resolution of a day. You can move the brush handles to adjust the window.


The counts of analogues retrieved for the selected period of interest are binned and displayed by decade, correlation and distance.


Methods:

We assume that a trajectory of a dynamical system (or an ensemble of trajectories) is observed during a reference period R, and that this trajectory is a good proxy for the underlying attractor. For a distinct target time interval T, we determine, for each t in T, the states of the reference trajectory in R that are closest to the state reached at target time t in T. The closest states found during the reference R are called the analogues of the target T. There are several ways of obtaining closest analogues: by minimizing a distance or maximizing a correlation. The analogue determination hence strongly depends on the criterion to be optimized and there is no objective reason to prefer one method over another [Toth, 1991a], although the results can be different.

Analogues from the A2C2 project use the standard Euclidean distance and linear correlation that are adapted to mean variations to select the first 20 "best" analogues. In the Analogues Detection process, the number of analogues to search for is user-defined, and the distance metric can be Euclidean, Mahalanobis, or cosine.

See the A2C2 website for more information.
